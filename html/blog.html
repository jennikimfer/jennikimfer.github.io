7/14/2023 - differentiating between nostalgia and abandonment

holding onto ghosts

several years ago, the "ghost trend" emerged on tiktok, where people would cover long lost friends on older group photos with a hand drawn picture of a ghost. albeit cute, when i first saw this, i thought it was ridiculous -- a strange obsession with holding onto people whose hands you had already let go of. it felt like a declaration of unrequited love for those who had abandoned you or a relinquishment of hatred for those who had wronged you. no matter the case, if that person was truly gone from your life, why would you continue to let them haunt you?

even the process of creating such a tiktok seemed inherently harmful. you would dig through photos of happy memories to spite those who had left you. in no way could it be interpreted positively. if it truly was for some greater, uplifting meaning, why would you cover their face? all it does is taint the good of the past with the bad of the now.

so, what could possibly be the benefit of participating in the "ghost trend"? do you feel some strange sense of popularity to show off all the non friends in your life? does it feel righteous to know those you blotted out of the photos will see that you have erased their personal significance?

i really, really, really thought about this one. the best answer i could come up with was that you feel satisfied to no longer attach people you associate with negativity in positive events.

the premise? sure, relatable to millions of teenagers who feel as if their voices are not heard, who feel as if their frustration is not seen, who feel as if their concerns are not validated. but... do we really need the "bunny girl senpai" of it all? cope.
